Classics are everyday meals I would make during the week that take anywhere from 10-60 minutes to prep,cook, and serve!

Stir Fry:

  • Prep:
    • 1/3lb of ground turkey or 1 Chicken Breast
    • 1/2 Bell Pepper
    • 1/2 Brocolli Crown
    • Rice
    • 1/2 of tsp diced Ginger&Garlic
    • Teriyaki Sauce
    • Rice Vinegar, Sesame Oil, and Olive Oil
  • Cook:
    1. Start cooking Rice in a pot or rice cooker
    2. Heat a large pan and drizzle two tbsp of olive oil and cook brocolli with lid
    3. After a 1-2 minutes start cooking the giner&garlic in a small pot or pan until starting to turn golden brown then put your turkey in and now put the rest of your veggies in the brocolli pan.
    4. Once turkey is about done pour about 1/4 cup (or more if you like it saucy) of sauce into veggies and stir
    5. Now add turkey to the veggies and stir again.
    6. Add about a 1/2-1 tsp of rice vinegar and sesame oil to the sauce and stir(be sparing it can overpower the dish easy!)
    7. The rice should be done by now and now add this to the sauce and stir.
  • Serve: Put everything on a plate and add green onions and sesasme seeds!
  • Tips:
    • If you don't have or don't want to buy rice vinegar use about 1/3 of a lemon's juice.
    • Don't overcook the garlic it will burn fast!

Ground Turkey with Mint&Rosemary Rice and Brocoli with a Balsamic Reduction:

  • Prep:
    • 1/3lb of Ground Turkey
    • .4-.75 cup of uncooked rice
    • brocolli
    • balsamic vinegar
    • 1tbsp of diced shallots
  • Cook:
    1. Sautee shallots in a pot until golden brown then pour balsamic in and reduce to about 1/8th ammount you put in. (Usually 20-35 min)
    2. After about 10 min of reducing balsamic, place your rice in pot or rice cooker with water and sprinkle rosemary and mint in and mix and then start cooking rice
    3. After about 5-10 minutes put chopped brocolli in a pan and place a lid over it.
    4. After cooking brocolli for 7-10 min start cooking turkey in a separate pan
    5. After turkey is done everything should be done or close!
  • Serve: Place everything on a plate or mix into a bowl and your ready to eat!
  • Tips:
    • If using a rice cooker after the rice finishes cooking take lid off and stir rice and place lid back on and let it steam another 4-7 min
    • Flip brocolli every few minutes to prevent it from burning
    • Depending on how much balsamic you use the time cooking will vary but if you do about half a medium pot full after reducing it will last you all week!
    • If you don't want to cook balsamic I will use hot sauce as a topping most night if I don't have an reduced balsamic on hand

Overeasy Omelot with Toast:

  • Prep:
    • 3 Large Eggs
    • 2 Pieces of Toast
    • Jelly, Almond Butter, or Peanut Butter
  • Cook:
    1. Heat up pan and crack 3 eggs into pan and place a lid or aluminum foil over pan
    2. Put toast in toaster
    3. Cook Eggs to preffered doneness
  • Serve: Put toppings on toast and you ready to serve!
  • Tips:
    • After 1-2 min of cooking eggs remove lid and use a fork and gently scrape at clear egg white around yolk to prevent uncooked egg white around yolk!
    • My favorite thing on toast right now is Mara Nathans Dark Chocolate Almond Butter
